Breakout Strategy Backtest

Breakout Trading Strategy

This page presents a complete breakout trading assignment built around a long-only Donchian-style breakout rule, a rolling walk-forward backtest, a downloadable trade blotter, a daily ledger, and out-of-sample performance analysis.

Strategy Logic

The strategy buys an asset when its closing price finishes above the highest high from the prior 20 trading days, which is intended to capture the start of a bullish trend. Trades are entered at the next day’s open, risk is controlled with a stop-loss placed 2.0 ATR below the entry price, profits are taken at 2.0 ATR above the entry price, and any position that remains open for 15 trading days is closed at the next open. The backtest uses one year of training followed by one quarter of out-of-sample testing, then rolls that process forward across the sample.

Breakout Detection Function

The core detection function is named detect_breakouts. It computes a shifted rolling high and flags a breakout only when the current close is above the highest high from the previous lookback window, which avoids look-ahead bias.

def detect_breakouts(data: pd.DataFrame, breakout_lookback: int) -> pd.DataFrame:
    breakout_frame = data.copy()
    breakout_frame["rolling_high"] = (
        breakout_frame["high"].rolling(window=breakout_lookback).max().shift(1)
    )
    breakout_frame["breakout_signal"] = (
        breakout_frame["close"] > breakout_frame["rolling_high"]
    ) & breakout_frame["rolling_high"].notna()
    return breakout_frame

All thresholds are explicitly defined in the analysis code:

  • BREAKOUT_LOOKBACK = 20 after walk-forward selection from {10, 20, 55}
  • ATR_WINDOW = 14
  • STOP_LOSS_ATR_MULTIPLE = 2.0
  • PROFIT_TARGET_ATR_MULTIPLE = 2.0
  • MAX_HOLD_DAYS = 15
  • RISK_FREE_RATE = 0.02

Asset Selection

I screened the ETF universe SPY, QQQ, IWM, XLE, GLD, and TLT using a rolling walk-forward framework with 252 trading days of training and 63 trading days of out-of-sample testing. XLE was selected because it delivered the strongest out-of-sample Sharpe ratio in the screen. The top three assets by walk-forward Sharpe were XLE, QQQ, and IWM.

Data retrieval is implemented with a ShinyBroker-first loader. The code first tries to request daily history through shinybroker.fetch_historical_data(...) so it can use a local IBKR/TWS session when one is available. In this run, there was no active local TWS connection, so the loader automatically fell back to a public Yahoo chart endpoint. That fallback is documented in the notebook and code so the workflow remains reproducible.

Breakout Definition

The breakout rule is:

Close_t > max(High_{t-20}, ..., High_{t-1})

In plain English, a breakout occurs when today’s closing price is above the highest daily high from the previous 20 sessions. The rolling high is shifted by one day so the strategy only uses information that would have been known at the close. ATR uses a 14-day window. The stop-loss is entry price - 2.0 * ATR, the profit target is entry price + 2.0 * ATR, and the timeout rule closes any open trade after 15 trading days.

Trade Outcome Analysis

Every completed trade is classified into one of three mutually exclusive outcomes:

  • Successful: the close reached the profit target and the strategy exited at the next open
  • Timed out: the trade remained open for 15 trading days and was closed with a market order at the next open
  • Stop-loss triggered: the close fell to or below the stop-loss threshold and the strategy exited at the next open

Interpretation

The final out-of-sample result is mixed rather than spectacular, which is useful in its own right. XLE produced the best walk-forward Sharpe in the ETF screen, but the strategy still had a modest average trade return and a non-trivial drawdown. That is a realistic outcome for a simple breakout rule: the framework is transparent, easy to audit, and capable of catching trends, but it is still sensitive to whipsaws and the exact exit logic. For a future extension, I would test volume confirmation, volatility filters, and a wider asset universe.
